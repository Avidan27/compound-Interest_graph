❓ Common Questions
Frequently Asked Questions
Everything a college student needs to know before their first investment.

Money sitting in a savings account earns ~3% — but inflation in India runs at ~5-6%. That means every year you don't invest, you're actually losing purchasing power. Investing is how you:

  • Beat inflation and grow real wealth over time
  • Build financial independence before your 40s
  • Create passive income that funds your dreams
  • Achieve goals like travel, home ownership, or starting a business

Time is your biggest advantage as a student. Starting at 22 vs 32 can mean a 3x difference in final wealth.

The stock market is where long-term wealth is created in India. The Nifty 50 index has returned ~14-16% CAGR over the past decade — far outpacing FDs and savings accounts. As a student:

  • You have time to ride out market volatility (short-term dips don't hurt you)
  • Even small SIPs of ₹500/month can compound to lakhs over 10-15 years
  • Understanding markets builds financial literacy that will serve you for life
  • Many companies offer ESOP (stock options) — knowing how stocks work helps you negotiate better

You don't need to pick stocks — just invest in a Nifty 50 index fund via SIP and let compounding do the work.

All platforms below are SEBI-regulated and trusted by millions of Indians:

  • Groww — Best for beginners. Clean UI, stocks, MFs, FDs, Gold. No minimum for most products.
  • Zerodha — India's largest broker. Best for serious equity investors. Kite is excellent.
  • Jar App — Micro-investing in digital gold. Auto-invests spare change from UPI payments.
  • Upstox — Low brokerage, great for ETFs and mutual funds. Tiger Global backed.
  • ET Money — Excellent for mutual fund SIPs and tax planning (ELSS for 80C savings).
  • RBI Retail Direct — Free platform to invest directly in Government bonds. Zero commission.

Start with Groww or ET Money if you're a beginner — both are extremely user-friendly for students.

Gold is a great diversifier — not a primary growth engine. Here's the breakdown:

  • Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) — Best form of gold. Earns 2.5% interest on top of price appreciation. Zero storage cost. Issued by RBI.
  • Digital Gold (Groww, Jar) — Convenient, start from ₹1. But no extra interest like SGB.
  • Gold ETFs — Listed on exchange, liquid, low expense ratio. Good for trading.

Ideal allocation: 5–10% of your portfolio in gold as a hedge against inflation and economic uncertainty. Don't make it your primary investment.

Follow the simple 50-30-20 rule with your pocket money or part-time income:

  • 50% on needs (food, transport, textbooks)
  • 30% on wants (entertainment, eating out)
  • 20% on saving + investing

If you earn ₹10,000/month from a part-time job, that's ₹2,000 for investing. Even ₹500/month in a Nifty 50 index fund via SIP makes a difference. The habit matters more than the amount early on.

Market crashes are temporary — and actually a student's best friend! Here's why:

  • Crashes are when your SIP buys more units at lower prices (rupee cost averaging)
  • Every historical market crash (2008, 2020 COVID) was followed by full recovery and new highs
  • As a student, your investment horizon is 20-30 years — short-term volatility is irrelevant
  • Never panic-sell. The worst thing you can do is exit at the bottom

Think of a crash as a "sale" on great assets. Keep your SIP running and let time work its magic.

SIP = Systematic Investment Plan. It auto-deducts a fixed amount monthly from your bank and invests it in a mutual fund. Starting a SIP takes 5 minutes:

  • Download Groww or ET Money
  • Complete KYC (PAN + Aadhaar, 100% digital)
  • Search for a Nifty 50 Index Fund (e.g., UTI Nifty 50 Index Fund)
  • Set monthly SIP amount (minimum ₹100–500 on most funds)
  • Set auto-debit date — done! Your money invests itself every month

No need to time the market. Just start and be consistent. That's the real secret.
